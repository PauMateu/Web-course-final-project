Some cocktail facts...

Biggest Cocktail Ever!

In 1694, Admiral Edward Russell created the world’s largest cocktail of 250 gallons of brandy, 125 gallons of Malaga wine, 1,400 pounds of sugar, 2,500 lemons, 20 gallons of lime juice, and 5 pounds of nutmeg, in a punch bowl so large it had to be served by a boy rowing across it.

Cock-tail!

The word “cocktail” comes from a practice of horse dealers in the 1700s. To cock a horse’s tail and make it look more spirited for the show, the dealer would give it a ginger suppository. Adding spices like ginger or pepper to gin or beer became known as adding “cock-tail”.

Prank gone wrong!

The Tom Collins cocktail is named after an 1874 prank in which someone would be led on a wild goose chase from bar to bar by friends, being told a libelous fellow named Tom Collins was besmirching their name. Newspapers bought it too.
